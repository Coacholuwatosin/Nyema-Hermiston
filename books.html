The Books

Evidence-based guides for parents and practitioners — written from thirty years of clinical experience and a commitment to honest, unfiltered research.

Book One

Planning Parenthood in the Age of Autism

A research-based guide for parents who want to make informed choices about pregnancy and neurodevelopmental health. Nyema draws on environmental medicine, nutritional science, and developmental biology to give parents the full picture of what influences neurodevelopment from the moment of conception.

This is the book that mainstream perinatal care rarely provides. Written for parents who are willing to ask harder questions and act on the answers.

Key Topics

Preconception Health Environmental Toxins Neurodevelopment Nutritional Science Gut Health Vaccine Considerations

Book Two

Treat Your Child's Ear Infections Yourself

A practical guide for parents on how to safely manage ear pain, infections, and fevers at home before turning to antibiotics. Grounded in clinical evidence and Nyema's decades of paediatric practice, this book gives parents the knowledge and confidence to make truly informed decisions.

Covers when home treatment is appropriate, which natural remedies have the strongest evidence base, and when to seek further medical care.

Book Three

Treat Your Child Yourself

Simple, safe, and effective drug-free solutions for over 100 common childhood ailments, co-authored with Jon Gamble. This comprehensive home reference guides parents through the management of everything from coughs and colds to digestive complaints — without defaulting to pharmaceutical intervention as a first response.

An essential reference for every parent who wants to be genuinely informed and genuinely empowered.

Book Four

Good News for People with Bad News

Recovery stories from 41 interviews with people across six countries who used dynamic therapies to treat severe health conditions. This book does not offer promises. It offers documented evidence of what is possible when patients and practitioners look beyond the conventional playbook.

Honest, rigorous, and profoundly human — a book for anyone who has been told there is nothing more that can be done.
